## Understanding Your Finances

Start by listing income, recurring expenses, and liabilities to create a clear picture of cash flow; calculate a simple emergency buffer goal (e.g., 3 months of essential expenses). Use a monthly snapshot to understand where money flows and identify quick wins.

## Budgeting Basics

Choose a budgeting method that fits your life (zero-based, percentage-based, or envelope system) and automate savings and bill payments where possible to reduce decision fatigue.

### Tracking Expenses

Track expenses for one month to categorize spending and identify areas to optimize; use a spreadsheet or a lightweight app and review categories weekly.

### Setting Financial Goals

Set SMART financial goals (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound): short-term (pay off small debt), medium (build emergency fund), and long-term (retirement contributions), and review progress quarterly.
